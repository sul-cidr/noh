Two stage assistants bring in a tsukurimono, a rectangular shape covered with a curtain, and place in front of the two percussionists on Square 8. It is later revealed that the shite is hidden inside of it. The prop represents a dilapidated dwelling with a thatched roof. Its symbolic entrance gate is hung with a hashitomi or hajitomi, the source of the play's title. Hashitomi means ‘half shitomi’, an oblong wooden panel shutter, the upper edge of which is hinged and the loose lower end can be pushed open. Yūgao evening flowers and gourds creep over it.

Two stage assistants create mystery when they slowly and carefully bring onstage an unusual tsukurimono, a rectangular shape covered by a curtain that will represent Lady Yūgao's dwelling.
