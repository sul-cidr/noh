Form
First Act - Shite Exits:
Kakaru-2 → Ageuta-1
Synopsis
"The yūgao flower from Gojō" is the final hint about the identity of the apparition, who then slowly disappears into the shadow of the flowers.
Text
Poetry in regular rhythm.
Music
The jiutai makes its entrance singing in yowagin style, a chant set in hiranori. It is accompanied by percussion in strict rhythmic setting and a non-congruent nohkan part.
Visuals
The use of stage space is expanded when the shite performs a series of standard formal kata within Squares 1 and 2.

The first Ageuta is the dramatic and intermedia climax not only of the Shite Exits dan, but of the whole first act. It brings a new and refreshing sound: the jiutai, the last performer who had not yet taken part in the play. The jiutai’s first entrance always delineates an important moment in a play, hence it's special name: shodō. The Ageuta is also the first shōdan accompanied by the full hayashi – the two percussion instruments and the nohkan. It produces the richest and most rhythmically consolidated texture so far with the chant set in hiranori accompanied by the percussion in strict rhythmic setting.
