Form
Second Act - Shite Re-enters:
Issei → Sageuta → Ageuta-2
Synopsis
While poetry is sung the Yūgao spirit reveals itself.
Text
Poetry in regular rhythm.
Music
The chant by the jiutai set in hiranori is accompanied by percussion instruments in strict rhythmic setting.
Visuals
The focus is on the dwelling located on Square 8, where halfway through the shōdan the drape is slowly removed and the shite is revealed. However, the shite remains partly obstructed by the lattice gate of the tsukurimono.

In the preceding two shōdan, the audience only hears a voice from the tsukurimono. Now, when the drape around the tsukurimono is removed the spirit finally shows itself. The revealing is gradual. The lowering of the drape is slow and the actor still remains partly concealed by a lattice gate.

The expression of the moment is colored by the melancholy of the text, sung by the jiutai, which speaks of a limpid moon casting its light on a zither stand. The words that follow refer to the autumn evening. It is a metaphor for the 'beginning of the end' and a variation on the theme of sadness already introduced in the previous two shōdan.

This coincides with the first mimetic movement of the second act. In a context of minimal motion, small gestures gain importance. As the jiutai sings “beyond the hedge, the autumn mountains”, the shite, who is still sitting in the tsukurimono, slides to the right as if to better admire them. The feeling of sorrow lingers through the last line : such a desolate evening!.
