Form
First Act - Shite Exits:
Kakaru-2 → Ageuta-1
Synopsis
"The yūgao flower from Gojō" is the final hint about the identity of the apparition, who then slowly disappears into the shadow of the flowers.
Text
Poetry in regular rhythm.
Music
The jiutai makes its entrance singing in yowagin style, a chant set in hiranori. It is accompanied by percussion in strict rhythmic setting and a non-congruent nohkan part.
Visuals
The use of stage space is expanded when the shite performs a series of standard formal kata within Squares 1 and 2.

The first Ageuta is the dramatic and intermedia climax not only of the Shite Exits dan, but of the whole first act. It brings a new and refreshing sound: the jiutai, the last performer who had not yet taken part in the play. The jiutai’s first entrance always delineates an important moment in a play, hence it's special name: shodō. The Ageuta is also the first shōdan accompanied by the full hayashi – the two percussion instruments and the nohkan. It produces the richest and most rhythmically consolidated texture so far with the chant set in hiranori accompanied by the percussion in strict rhythmic setting.

The narrative introduces an important piece of information about the main character's identity. In the very first line, it gives the location of her dwelling in Gōjo. According to the Genji monogatari, this location is associated with Lady Yūgao’s home. It is standard for an Ageuta’s first line to be repeated, a repetition that underlines the text. Moreover, it is also standard to separate the initial statement and its repetition with an uchikiri that temporarily interrupts the narrative, creating a ma moment that emphasizes the repeated line: “Near Gojō, she said, of the ‘evening face.’”.

The final passage involves stage movement related to the text. While the jiutai sings, “In the moment the eyes turn aside, she becomes a dream,” the shite takes Six Steps Forward, in preparation for a Right Circlet, performed to mimic the woman’s disappearance, as announced by the jiutai: “…as she disappears in the shadow of the flowers..” As the melodic line, supported by the nohkan’s mi roku no ge pattern, moves from the high range to medium, preparing to cadence in the low range, the shite performs the standard closing pair of movements Forward Pointing and Open-retreat. Then, the shite slowly leaves the stage in complete silence.
