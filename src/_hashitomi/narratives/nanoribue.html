Form
First Act - Waki Enters:
Nanoribue → Nanori → Kakaru-1
Synopsis
Arrival of a monk.
Text
n/a
Music
Nohkan solo.
Visuals
The waki dressed in humble monk clothes enters the stage and stops at Square 1. He is followed by the kyōgen in a local townsman costume, who sits down at the back of the bridge opposite Pine 1. The kyōgen usually enter inconspicuously in the middle of the first act during the first ageuta. In Hashitomi, the first ageuta concludes the act and is followed immediately by the shite’s exit. Thus, the kyōgen enters earlier, inconspicuously, together with the waki, so as not to interfere with the shite's exit.

The play opens with the entrance of the monk accompanied by a nohkan solo. The nohkan player performs the solo in a plain fashion, which underlines the modesty of the monk, and introduces the play’s lighter tone. The monk's monochromatic costume gives an sense of subdued and subtle elegance.

The simple instrumentation of this unmetered melody, the absence of text, and the to-be-expected simple walk-in of the actor provides this shōdan with the play's lowest Intermedia Index, a common way of starting the first shōdan of a play’s first dan.

It is the nohkan player’s responsibility to loosely adapt the pacing of the melody to the waki’s walking speed so that the conclusion of the entrance music coincides with the waki’s final kata : the Step back.
