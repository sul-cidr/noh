Form
Second Act - Shite Re-enters:
Issei → Sageuta → Ageuta-2
Synopsis
Continuation of poetry heard from the inside of the tsukurimono, now invoking a metaphor of tears by referring to snow-dampened sleeves.
Text
Poetry in regular rhythm.
Music
The chant of the shite set in hiranori is accompanied by the percussion in strict rhythmic setting.
Visuals
There is no conspicuous movement on stage among actors. The attention of the audience remains to be drawn to the symbolic dwelling from within which the shite continues to sing.

With its text set in hiranori accompanied percussion instruments in strict rhythm, the Sageuta is the first of a sequence of five consecutive congruent shōdan. The text of the second act has now shifted from prose to verse.

A nostalgic tone is introduced. It started with the preceding shōdan’s last line : “Rain dampens Genken's (Yüan Hsien) gate,” in which rain is a metaphor for tears. It continues in the Sageuta which starts with another metaphor for tears: dampened sleeves . This is carried over to the next shōdan, the Ageuta with a reference to the limpid moon soaring over a desolate evening. The source of the nostalgia is only revealed in the following shōdan – the Rongi, when the shite asks “ When will they meet again?,” a clear and poignant reference to a lover's yearning.

The positioning of this short chant between the Issei-chant and the Ageuta is easier to appreciate when the viewer considers the shape of the vocal line across the three shōdan. The Sageuta begins in the low register, completing the descending trajectory from high to medium of the Issei-chant, before moving up towards the medium range, thus serving as a transition towards the high range of the coming Ageuta.
