The hayashi and jiutai slowly walk in and take their positions in the back and on stage-left, respectively.

The entrance of the musicians happens in silence. For the instrumentalists, the curtain is pulled back partially on one side, and then lifts fully during the actorsâ€™ dramatized arrival. Since the musicians' role is accompanying, they appear with humility and poise. They enter one at a time and walk slowly over the bridge to their standard position. The jiutai members enter through a small door on stage-left, and with same modesty and decorum, take their seated positions to the side of the stage. The performers avoid turning their backs to the audience or making eye contact. They preserve the kurai, introduced during the oshirabe. After their arrival, an assistant to the kotsuzumi player enters through the jiutai door and sits down behind the master.
