The machiutai is a type of ageuta known as a “waiting song.” Set in hiranori, it features a melody of six or seven lines sung in a high range by the waki at the beginning of the second act, while he waits for the shite to re-enter the stage. Its musical structure is identical to that of the ageuta, but unlike the typically poetic text of the ageuta, the machiutai presents a factual narrative.
