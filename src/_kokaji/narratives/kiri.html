Form
Second Act - Shite Exits:
Kakeai-2 → Kiri
Synopsis
The sword is named 'Clustering Clouds of Heaven' and its power celebrated with a dance. The deity proclaims the end of its work, offers the sword to the Imperial envoy and jumps on a mass of clouds to return to Mt. Inari.
Text
Poetry in irregular rhythm.
Music
A tsuyogin style chant of the jiutai with one intervention by the shite set in chūnori and hiranori, accompanied by kotsuzumi and ōtsuzumi in strict and nohkan in non-congruent rhythmic settings.
Visuals
A dance-to-text using most of the stage space. The shite takes the blade, performs a few kata holding it prominently then brings it to the envoy while the waki watches them. The envoy accepts it, the shite bows and performs a short sequence of kata ending with a rapid walk onto the bridge stopping at the Second Pine where he mimes his disappearance.

The final shōdan of the play involves active dance, congruent singing, strict percussion and striking closing pattern by the nohkan. This brings the Intermedia Index to its second highest point. The mimetic movement matches the text, and the fast and intense music underlines the climactic character of the section.

The kiri can be divided into three parts: introduction of the sword as 'Clustering Clouds of Heaven', boasting of its powers as it is offered to the envoy, and the deity’s exit.

To proclaim the name of the sword with emphasis, a four-beat toriji measure is inserted. It interrupts the steady flow of honji and allows to precede the name “Ama no murakumo…” (Clustering Clouds of Heaven...)” with a pause that precisely stresses these words. The sword is demonstrated proudly by the deity by performing the standard sequence of kata: Forward Pointing and Open-retreat, while holding the blade up high at arm's length.

Second part begins with the deity looking at the sword held between stretched arms and pronouncing: 'First under heaven'. After repeating the claim the jiutai tells about the protection and blessings that the sword will bring. The dance’s momentum picks up and it will not abate until the end of the kiri. The shite starts by reinforcing the statements with a series of powerful Six and Seven Stamps that produces a dynamic counterpoint with the melodic line. The text that follows speaks of ruling the land to the four seas and abundance of the five staple crops. It is mimicked with the loosely synchronized sequence of formal kata: Pointing and Large Circle . As the jiutai sings “Inari no shintai Kogitsune-maru wo/The manifestation of Inari: the Little Fox”, the shite prepares to formally deliver the blade by performing an Open-retreat that brings him back to Square 1. In a moment filled with intensity, the deity holding the sword with two hands, walks forward to the Emperor's envoy, hands it to him, and moves back to Kneel and Bow.

The final section of the text starts with " kore made nari to ii-sutete/Now, I am done!", and brings a change of tone created by a sudden spike in tempo of both music and dance. The deity stands up and performs several virtuosic kata with spectacular agility: Two Large Steps, Leap Turn, and a sequence of Kneeling that mimic the words “mata mura-kumo ni tobi-nori/he leaps upon the mass of clouds. The closing nohkan’s tome no te pattern confirms the finality of the situation as the deity moves with determination to the 2nd Pine on the Bridge.Again he takes Two Large Steps in preparation for a Leap Turn, illustrating his return to Mt. Inari, over the words “Higashiyama Inari no mine ni zo kaerikeru/and returns to the Eastern Peak of Mt. Inari!. Finally, the deity flips his Left sleeve over his head to indicate disappearance from view, while Munechika keeps his gaze into the distance, as if seeing the diety off.
