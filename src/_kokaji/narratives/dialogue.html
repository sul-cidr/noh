First Act - Waki Enters:
Nanori → Mondō-1 → Ageuta-1 → Dialogue
Synopsis
Under pressure from the Imperial envoy, Munechika accepts to forge the blade.
Text
Prose.
Music
Unaccompanied exchange between the waki and wakizure delivered in kotoba.
Visuals
The waki is keeling at Square 9 and wakizure at Square 5, where he will stay until the end of the play. Except for the moment at the very end when he will received the newly forged blade, he should be considered absent.

This short two-line dialogue is an introduction to the waki’s monologue, which follows. It includes the envoy’s last line in the play, telling Munechika to move on with forging the requested blade. The swordsmith acquiesces.

The return to unmetered and unaccompanied prose brings the Intermedia Index back down. The energy of the dialogue is also constrained by the kneeling position and minimal movement of the two actors. Being the first out of three spoken shōdan, the musicians and members of the jiutai put aside their instruments and fans, respectively. Because the waki’s role in Kokaji is substantial and will involve further movement across parts of the stage, it is the wakizure who sits at the waki’s pole (Square 5), where he will remain until the end of the play. Wakizure can be assumed absent from the stage until the final scene when he is briefly needed to accept the sword.
