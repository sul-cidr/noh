First Act - Waki Enters:
Nanori → Mondō-1 → Ageuta-1 → Dialogue → Monologue
Synopsis
Under pressure from the Imperial envoy, Munechika accepts the mandate to forge the blade.
Text
Prose.
Music
Unaccompanied exchange between the waki and wakizure delivered in kotoba.
Visuals
The waki is keeling at Square 9 and the wakizure at Square 5, where he will stay until the end of the play. Except for the moment at the very end when he receives the newly forged blade, he is considered absent and invisible.

This dialogue, composed of two lines, leads to the waki’s monologue. It includes the envoy’s last line in the play, which tells Munechika to forge the requested blade. The swordsmith acquiesces.

The return to unmetered and unaccompanied prose lowers the Intermedia Index. The two actors' kneeling position and minimal movement further constrains the dialogue's energy. Since this is the first out of three spoken shōdan, the musicians and members of the jiutai put aside their instruments and fans. Because the waki’s role in Kokaji is substantial and will involve further movement across parts of the stage, it is the wakizure who sits at the waki’s pole (Square 5), where he will remain until the end of the play. From this point on, and until he is briefly needed at the end of the play to accept the sword, the wakizure is considered absent and invisible.
