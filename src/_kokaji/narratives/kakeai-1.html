First Act - Shite Exits:
Kakeai-1 → Ageuta-3 → Raijo
Synopsis
Munechika acknowledges the encouragement he has drawn from hearing about the legendary Chinese and Japanese swords. He asks the young boy to reveal his identity but is told instead to pray, prepare for the forging, and wait.
Text
Prose.
Music
Exchange between the shite and the waki delivered in kotoba accompanied by percussion in flexible rhythmic setting. Final few words are sung in non-congruent setting in tsuyogin style.
Visuals
The shite and waki are back to their initial kneeling positions on Square 9 and 6, respectively. The shite turns toward waki for the last two lines of the text.

The shōdan marks a sharp return to one of the simplest textures: recitation of prose accompanied by the percussion instruments in flexible rhythmic setting.

The two protagonists return to their respective kneeling positions in Squares 9 and 6 and face each other. Although the swordsmith tries one last time to inquire about the boy’s identity, the latter brushes aside the question and tells Munechika to get his forge ready. Then, raising on a knee and switching to chanting, thus producing a commanding tone, he dramatically tells Munchika to expect his return.
