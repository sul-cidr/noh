Form
Second Act - Shite Exits:
Kakeai-2 → Kiri
Synopsis
Munechika and the deity complete the forging of the blade by inscribing their names; Munechika the Swordsmith on one side, and Little Fox on the other.
Text
Prose.
Music
An exchange between shite and waki delivered in kotoba accompanied by ōtsuzumi and kotsuzumi in flexible rhythmic setting.
Visuals
The two protagonists remain on the dais located on Square 4 and mimick inscribing their names on the blade with the hammers.

The Intermedia Index drops significantly. Musically the kakeai-2 is like a moment of respite before the play's dynamic conclusion. Thus, for the span of three honji, there is a substantial reduction of density in the musical textture with the absence of the nohkan, taiko, and jiutai. Moreover, the text, which is factual, is spoken by the shite and waki, rather than sung. Finally, the strict rhythmic synchronization of the chant and its accompaniment found in the preceding five shōdan is temporarily interrupted, as it will return in the next and last shōdan, the kiri.

Yet, when it comes to the coordination between text and kata, the kakeai-2 is very much a continuation of the noriji-3. One hears and sees the two performers completing the forging of the blade by engraving their respective signature.
