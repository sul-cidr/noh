Form
First Act - Shite Performs:
Kuri → Sashi → Kuse
Synopsis
The young boy tells the story of a late minister's soul giving power to the emperor Huan Tsung's blade.
Text
Arhythmic poetry.
Music
The shite’s chant is followed by the jiutai’s, both sung in tsuyogin style of a text set in sashinori. They are accompanied by percussion in flexible and the nohkan in non-congruent rhythmic settings.
Visuals
The shite and the waki are kneeling on Squares 9 and 6, respectively. For the final line of text they turn toward each other after which, the shite turns toward the audience.

Although the shite joins the jiutai in singing, the Intermedia Index is lowered because the chant is in non-congruent sashinori, the percussion instruments switch back to flexible setting and nohkan part is non-congruent. The decrease has also to do with the absence of any stage movement as the two protagonists remain motionless, almost until the end of the shōdan.

The text narrates the story of another ancient Chinese sword, and while the shite and jiutai alternate in singing, this is not a dialogue since the jiutai simply carries on the boy’s words. While the text of the next shōdan will focus on a legendary Japanese sword, the sashi’s text concludes the narration of stories related to Chinese swords started in the preceding kuri. The shōdan closes with an uptick of energy when the nohkan enters with the kuse no ryō pattern over the line “and their miraculous power is beyond expression! ”, as the shite turns to make eye contact with the waki.
