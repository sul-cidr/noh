Synopsis
A deity from a subsidiary shrine summarizes the events from the first act, gives additional accounts of famous swords, and expresses confidence in the power of prayer and consequently Munechika's ability to complete the task.
Text
Prose.
Music
None.
Visuals
The kyōgen actor wearing a traditional kyōgen garb for a subsidiary deity and a noborihige mask delivers his monologue standing at Square 1.

The kyōgen in Kokaji starts with the actor's entrance over the continuing music of raijo. This, and the fact that the actor wears a mask is somewhat rare and is dictated by the fact that the character is a diety. The intermedia character of the interlude is simple, as it is usually the case. The actor recites his text with very little movement and without music.

Often during the interlude, also called the nakairi, the kyōgen actor engages in a discussion with the waki. In such instances, he kneels center stage conversing with the waki kneeling at Square 5, as it is the case with Hashitomi’s aikyōgen. But in Kokaji, the waki has exited the stage with the shite for a change of costume, thus the actor addresses the audience with a monologue standing on Square 1. Like the members of the jiutai, the four musicians have their hands in their pockets to show that although on stage, they are not part of the action.
