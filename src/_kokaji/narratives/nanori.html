First Act - Waki Enters:
Nanori → Mondō-1 → Ageuta-1 → Dialogue
Synopsis
An envoy from Emperor Ichijo introduces himself as Tachibana no Michinari. He is traveling to inform the swordsmith Munechika of an imperial order requesting that he forges a blade that the emperor has seen in a dream oracle.
Text
Prose.
Music
Unaccompanied vocal part of the wakizure delivered in kotoba.
Visuals
Having walked through the bridge, the wakizure dressed in courtier’s costume stops at Square 1 to deliver his lines. The stage may represent the Imperial Palace where the envoy starts his journey or somewhere on the way to Kyoto where Munechika lives. His walking back onto the bridge stopping at the First Pine suggests that he has traveled and finally arrived at his destination.

The envoy's unaccompanied entrance is followed by his self-introduction and explanation of intentions, done at the quiet Square 1 in a simplest way as an unmetered, unaccompanied prose. Thus, the shōdan’s Intermedia Index is the play’s lowest, as it is commonly the case for a play’s first shōdan.

The envoy concludes the nanori by bringing his hands together, holding a fan in his right one. While the origin of the gesture is believed to be related to prayer, it has become a standard closing gesture used by secular and religious characters alike, such as the monk in Hashitomi.
