Form
First Act - Shite Exits:
Kakeai-1 → Ageuta-3 → Raijo
Synopsis
The young boy announces that after changing into a supernatural form, he will return to help Munechika and then, vanishes into the evening clouds of Mount Inari.
Text
Poetry in irregular rhythm.
Music
The text set in hiranori is sung by the jiutai in tsuyogin mode. The accompanying percussion is in strict rhythmic setting.
Visuals
In the first part the shite and the waki remain kneeling on Squares 9 and 6, respectively. The shite stands up and the waki bows to him. Then, the shite performs a brief dance on the stage’s right lateral and ends on Square 1.

This shōdan, the highlight of the Shite exits dan, substantially raises its Intermedia Index. It marks a return to poetic text set in hiranori accompanied by the percussion instruments in strict rhythmic setting. It also includes a short but dynamic dance.

The ageuta opens with the very dynamic kakegoe ‘iya’, which establishes an energetic tone that is maintained throughout the shōdan. Set in a fast tempo, the chant’s eight honji are all sung in the rhythmic tsuzuke-utai style. The performed kata are all formal but the text and context provide them with meaning. As we hear the jiutai speaking for the boy, saying that "I will come without fail, at the appointed time" the boy stands up and facing Munechika performs a Forward Pointing followed by an Open-retreat, two standard kata that visually reinforce his commitment. The swordsmith replies by ceremoniously bowing.

Then, as the jiutai refers to the clouds of Mount Inari where the boy is about to vanish, the tempo suddenly picks up. In preparation for his imminent departure into the sky, the boy builds tension by moving to the very dynamic Square 3, where he circles and faces the waki. Then he releases the tension by going to Square 1 where he performs a Circlet Point. It symbolizes his disappearance witnessed by Munechika who turns right, raises on his knee and points forward in the distance where, the young boy has disappeared.
