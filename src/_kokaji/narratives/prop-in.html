The tsukurimono is brought in by two stage assistants and placed on Square 4. It is a kind of dais decorated with ropes and paper offerings that represents Munechika's forge. Shortly after, the curtain is lifted and the swordsmith walks-in in silence. He enters the stage, stops for a moment on Square 2 facing the audience, turns slightly toward the dais as if acknowledging its presence and then, turns further away to walk to Square 8. There, he kneels down facing diagonally away from the audience making himself absent. He awaits the second act to begin.
