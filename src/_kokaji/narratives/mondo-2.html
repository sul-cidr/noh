Form
First Act - Dialogue:
Mondō-2 → Ageuta-2
Synopsis
As Munechika walks to pray at the Inari Shrine for divine assistance in forging the blade, a mysterious young boy appears out of nowhere and surprises Munechika with the knowledge of the Imperial order.
Text
Prose.
Music
Unaccompanied exchange between the shite and waki delivered in kotoba.
Visuals
The waki standing on Square 6 turns toward the mirror room whose curtain is lifted, when he hears the voice of the shite, who enters the bridge and walks to Square 1.

Although the Intermedia Index of this spoken dialogue is low, it has an interesting intermedia quality that comes from the combination of the use of stage space and text setting.

Kokaji’s first act does not have a Shite enters dan because his appearance is intended to be a surprise, an effect that would have been spoiled with an entrance music. Thus, the shōdan opens with the shite's first line of a dialogue. It comes as a call from the distance called yobikake, heard as the shite is still out of sight in the mirror room. The waki standing on Square 6 facing in his direction, is mesmerized, as he wonders about the origin of the heard voice. This leads to the entrance of the shite and an exchange between the two protagonists separated by the widest possible distance that creates a dramatic effect as if worlds apart.

As the shite in the role of a young boy finally reaches Square 1 and turns to face Munechika, there is a sudden change of tone and style in the delivery of the last two lines of the shōdan. The actors switch to non-congruent singing to share a sentence of poetic text. The waki starts with a line that summarizes his astonishment : "A voice in heaven". It is followed with the shite’s "echoes on the earth.", which confirms his arrival in Munechika’s world. The switch also serves as a smooth transition to the next shōdan, whose poetic text is also sung.
