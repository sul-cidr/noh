Form
Second Act - Shite Performs:
Noriji-2 → Maibataraki → Noriji-3
Synopsis
Munechika is called upon to start forging the blade.
Text
Poetry in irregular rhythm.
Music
The jiutai sings in tsuyogin style a text set in ōnori with the accompaniment of all three percussion instruments in strict and the nohkan in non-congruent rhythmic settings.
Visuals
Standing at the first pine, the shite performs a brief series of kata before entering the stage proper. He continues with a sequence of kata encompassing the middle and right latteral and concluding on Square 1.

The noriji-2 functions as an introduction to the dance. In fact, the noriji with its immutable honji of limited number of syllables, often leads to a dance, as it is the case here. The main feature of this shōdan is the shite’s grandiose entrance on the main stage supported by the full hayashi and the jiutai, who speaks for the deity.

Yet, the noriji-2 begins with an introduction that further delays the shite’s entrance on the main stage. Still standing at the First pine, the shite signals that he is about to make his move with a sequence of Six Stamps, that he concludes with a Left Turn, a Forward Pointing, and its usual companion kata, the Open-retreat, leading to the nohkan’s takane hane pattern, which concludes the introduction.

The shite finally enters the stage with a Large Zigzag performed in the dynamic Martial style. At the conclusion of this kata, the deity faces Munechika as the jiutai intones "The appointed time for forging is known", pressing him to get started with his task.

From this point on, the shite performs a sequence of kata that brings him to Square 1, where he concludes the shōdan with the standard sequence: Forward Pointing and Open-retreat.
