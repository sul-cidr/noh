Form
First Act - Shite Exits:
Rongi 1
Synopsis
The old couple engages in a dialogue, answering the priest's questions voiced by the juitai. In their exchange, the old couple identifies themselves as 'the paired pine gods of Takasago and Suminoe, appearing before you here as husband and wife.' The gods then invite the priest to travel to Sumiyoshi, they board the boat and leave.
Text
Poetry in irregular rhythm.
Music
Congruent chant of the juitai and the unison of shite and shitezure set in hiranori, sung in tsuyogin style. It is supported by a non-congruent nohkan part, and a percussion part set in strict setting.
Visuals
REDO
The shite, kneeling center stage at Square 9, slides left to face the waki as he delivers the line, '…all within the sovereign's land will share forever in the glory of the reign.' When the jiutai, speaking for the shite, confirms, 'To Sumiyoshi I will go now,' the shite stands up, moves to center stage at Square 4, and performs two silent, large steps to mimic his journey to Sumiyoshi. After that, the shite and shitezure walk over the bridge to exit the stage.

The Rongi is a chant in which the shite customarily alludes to or reveals his identity. For this reason, it typically concludes the first act, as is the case here. Ordinarily, a Rongi that closes the first act does not feature a part for the nohkan; however, in this instance, the Rongi does include this element. Based on the text, the shōdan may be divided into three sections. In the first, the couple discloses their identity; in the second, their happiness is presented as a consequence of a virtuous reign; and in the third, the topic shifts to their departure for Sumiyoshi.

Part I

This section features a statement by the jiutai, representing the waki, followed by a reply from the shite and shitezure. The jiutai’s passage serves as a celebration of the enduring union of the “Twin Pines.” The atypical repetition of the opening line, “these celebrated pine branches,” intensified by the nohkan’s takane pattern, underscores one of the central themes of the play. As the duo reveals their identity, the shite performs his first kata within this shōdan: rising onto his left knee from a kneeling position, he slides to his left to face the waki, thereby imparting a tone of solemnity to the revelation.

Part II

The reference to the “glory of the reign”—a theme revisited in the second act—is highlighted through two interconnected actions. As the jiutai sings, “All within the sovereign’s land will share forever in the glory of the reign,” the nohkan enters with its hishigi takane pattern, while the shite once again rises onto his left knee and slides left to face the waki. This sequence further elevates the significance of the statement.

Part III

As preparations are made for the departure to Sumiyoshi by boat, an atmosphere of excitement prevails, effectively conveyed by the unusual metric structure. The syllabic configuration of the third and fourth lines in this section is “Iunami no / migiwa naru (5/5) ama no obune ni / uchi norite (7/5)” (“So saying, where the evening waves meet the shore, he boards a fishing boat”). Typically, these two phrases would each match a honji. In this case, however, the first three syllables of the second hemistich, “ama no,” are joined to the previous phrase, altering the structure to “Iunami no / migiwa naru ama no (5/8) obune ni / uchi norite (4/5).” To accommodate the extended hemistich, an okuri (a two-beat measure) is inserted, creating a longer-than-usual pause reminiscent of ma, which serves to emphasize “obune,” the boat. This moment is further distinguished by the entry of the nohkan’s kuri age pattern.

Excitement about the impending journey to Sumiyoshi is also reflected in the sequence of kata. For instance, as the jiutai sings “Sumiyoshi ni mazu yukite” (“To Sumiyoshi I will go now”), the shite, facing the waki, raises his fan and loosens his grip to signify that the winds are favorable for the voyage. The shite then rises and moves to the front of the stage (Square 4), where right and left steps mimic the act of boarding the ship. This is succeeded by an open-retreat kata, with the shite's extended arms and lowered sleeves signifying the boat’s sail. His progression toward Square 1, accompanied by the nohkan’s roku no ge pattern, confirms his departure for Sumiyoshi.
