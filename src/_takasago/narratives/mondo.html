First Act - Dialogue:
Mondō → Kakeai → Ageuta 2 → Spoken
Synopsis
In this dialogue, the priest asks why the Takasago and Suminoe pines are considered a "pair," despite being geographically distant from one another. In response, the old man refers to them as the "Paired Pines," a term inspired by the preface of the Kokinshu, contemporary to the action, early Heian period anthology of waka poetry.
Text
Prose.
Music
Unaccompanied exchange between the shite and waki delivered in kotoba.
Visuals
Facing front, the shite and shitezure stand on Squares 8 and 2, respectively, while the waki stands at Square 5, facing the shite. The dialogue occurs exclusively between the shite and the waki, with the shite turning toward the waki when responding to his questions.
