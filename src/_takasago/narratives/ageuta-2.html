Form
First Act - Dialogue:
Mondō → Kakeai → Ageuta 2 → Dialogue
Synopsis
The text celebrates 'a sovereign whose blessings bring prosperity to all. In such times, the paired pines also rejoice.
Text
Poetic text in regular rhythm.
Music
This congruent chant by the jiutai is set in hiranori and sung in tsuyogin style. It is supported by a non-congruent nohkan part, and a percussion part in strict rhythmic setting.
Visuals
While the jiutai sings, the three actors reposition themselves. The shitezure moves to Square 7 and kneels, while the waki sits at Square 5. Meanwhile, the shite performs two Forward Points followed by Open-retreats, with the first one on the dominant Square 4, the next one on the weaker Square 1.

SOME NOTES:
• Besides the singing of the jitori in the shidai, this shōdan is marks the entrance of the jiutai.
• The preceding shōdan celebrated the love between the two pines
• Then, the kakeai paired Takasago with poetry, and Suminoe with the reign
• This shōdan focuses on the reign.
