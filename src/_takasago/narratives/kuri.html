Form
First Act - Shite Performs:
Kuri → Sashi 2 → Kuse
Synopsis
The jiutai sings that, although insentient, flowers and fruits recognize their seasons and bloom first on the western branches.
Text
Arhythmic poetry.
Music
This non-congruent chant by the jiutai is set in einori and sung in tsuyogin style. It is supported by a non-congruent nohkan part, and a percussion part in alternating strict and strict rhythmic settings.
Visuals
The shite moves to center stage at Square 9, where he faces front, sets down the rake, and instead takes a fan. As the jiutai intones 'nanshi hana…,' the shite and waki slide and turn to face each other. At the end of the shōdan, the shite slides back to face front.

Music plays a crucial role in all Kuri, the first of a three-shōdan structure, in which it is traditionally likened to the flow of water from a vigorous waterfall into the slower stream of the Sashi and finally to a tranquil pond in Kuse. Kuri showcases a great variety of fast-paced patterns, which can make it challenging to follow the text. All Kuri, including this one, conclude with yuri patterns of the nohkan, which, like the chanting in the final segment, are repetitive and melismatic, and create a feeling of extension or slowdown—even when the overall tempo remains active. Here, this is accompanied by the shite, who sets down the rake and takes out the fan as a signal that he is about to shift into a narrative mode. Together, the music and movement establish a smooth transition to the slower and more narratively perceptible Sashi that follows.

In the first category and other auspicious plays at higher kurai, the entrance of the jiutai is delayed after two or three honji to build anticipation and emphasize the importance of the Kuri.
