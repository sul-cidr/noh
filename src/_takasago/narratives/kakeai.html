Mondō → Kakeai → Ageuta 2 → Spoken
Synopsis
The old couple is depicted as a symbol of an auspicious reign. Takasago represents the ancient era of the Manyōshū, the oldest anthology of Japanese poetry, compiled around 759 AD, while Sumiyoshi symbolizes the present time and the reigning emperor of the Engi era.
Text
Prose before turning into arhythmic poetry.
Music
The vocal part starts in kotoba, before becoming a non-congruent chant sung in tsuyogin style set in sashinori. It is supported by a non-congruent nohkan part, and a percussion part in flexible setting.
Visuals
The shite and shitezure, standing on Squares 8 and 2, respectively, take turns answering the questions posed by the waki, who stands on Square 5.

TBD
The shite and tsure sing together twice when addressing the fact that they are referred to as a ‘pair’. These two references are sequentially supported by the entrance of the percussion instruments, and then, by the nohkan’s entrance. The core idea of the play is positioned in this shōdan, where alternating between singing and speaking Takasago is associated with poetry, Suminoe with the reign.
