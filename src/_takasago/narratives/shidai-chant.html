Form
First Act - Waki Enters:
Netori Okitsuzumi → Kaikō → Nanori → Shidai chant → Michiyuki → Tsuki-zerifu
Synopsis
The priest and the two attendants are ready to leave for the capital.
Text
Poetry in regular rhythm.
Music
During the short introductory instrumental segment, the nohkan part is non-congruent with the percussion in flexible setting. The percussion parts switch to strict setting at the end of the segment and remain so during the chant sung by the waki and two wakizure. The chant is sung in tsuyogin style set in hiranori.
Visuals
The waki standing at Square 5 faces the two wakizure standing at Squares 2 and 3.

In a typical Takasago performance, this chant appears at the beginning of the play, preceded by Shinno shidai music. In this case, however, there is only a short six-measure fragment of Shidai> music played, which is there for the actors to walk to their positions on stage: the waki on the right facing the attendants on the left. Traditionally, Shidai chant consists of the text being sung three times: first by the waki and wakizure, then very softly, omitting the repetition of the first line, by the jiutai, and finally once again by the waki and wakizure. The first two are done using mitsuji patterns in the percussion, leading to the third, which has an increased energy of the tsuzuke patterns.
