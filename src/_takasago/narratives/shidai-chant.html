Form
First Act - Waki Enters:
Netori Okitsuzumi → Kaikō → Nanori → Shidai chant → Michiyuki → Tsuki-zerifu
Synopsis
The priest and the two attendants are ready to leave for the capital.
Text
Poetry in regular rhythm.
Music
During the short introductory instrumental segment, the nohkan part is non-congruent with the percussion in flexible setting. The percussion parts switch to strict setting at the end of the segment and remain so during the chant sung by the waki and two wakizure. The chant is sung in tsuyogin style set in hiranori.
Visuals
The waki standing at Square 5 faces the two wakizure standing at Squares 2 and 3.

In a typical performance of Takasago, this chant opens the play, preceded by Shin-no shidai music. Here, however, only a brief six-measure fragment of Shidai is played, serving simply to allow the actors to take their positions: the waki on the right, facing the attendants on the left. Traditionally, the Shidai chant presents the text three times: first by the waki and wakizure; then, very softly and omitting the repeat of the first line, by the jiutai; and finally once more by the waki and wakizure. The first two iterations use mitsuji percussion patterns, leading to the third, which shifts to the more energetic tsuzuke patterns.
