Form
First Act - Shite Enters:
Ashirai → Song
Synopsis
The apparition refers poetically to the flower offerings in Buddhist service.
Text
Poetry in regular rhythm.
Music
Unaccompanied unmetered song of the shite set in sashinori and sung in yowagin style centered in the medium range.
Visuals
Shite is standing at the First Pine, facing the audience.

Based on a 9th-century tanka, the 'song' is the play’s first sung poetic text. The introduction of chant and poetic language raises the Intermedia Index. However, because it is performed without accompaniment, sung by the shite who remains motionless at the First Pine, the overall Intermedia Index stays relatively low.

The 'song' is a compelling example of Noh becoming a stage for the thoughts of the shite and waki, and a melting of distinctions between them. The poetic text sung by the woman refers to an offering of flowers to Buddha, alluding to the memorial service for flowers performed by the monk. One could speculate that her apparition voices the thoughts of the monk. Thus, the two characters have now become one despite their physical distance—the woman remaining on the bridge while the monk is sitting at the waki’s post (Square 5). The veiled dreamy quality of the scene is emphasized by the shite's slight lowering of the mask, a gesture called kumorasu – ‘cloud the face,’ often performed to suggest contemplation.
