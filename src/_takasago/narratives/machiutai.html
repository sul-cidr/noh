Form
Second Act - Waki Waits:
Machiutai
Synopsis
The priest and his two attendants travel on boat and arrive at Suminoe.
Text
Poetry in regular rhythm.
Music
The joint chant of waki and wakizure set in hiranori is sung in tsuyogin style. It is accompanied by the percussion in strict and nohkan in non-congruent rhythmic settings. In the final measure they are joined by the taiko drum.
Visuals
The two wakizure stand up, walk to Square 2 and 3 and turn to face the waki, who stood up at Square 5. After finishing the chant, they return to their previous seating positions.

This shōdan is the first in the second act and opens the Waki Waits dan. Because Takasago is a celebratory play, it begins with music. In other plays, the waki often starts alone and is later joined by the ensemble. The instrumental introduction is a relatively fast segment of four measures that culminates in a cadential uchikiri, accompanying the waki and Wwkizure as they stand and return to their positions from Act 1—in Squares 2, 3, and 5. This is a notable deviation, as they usually do not move in Act 2 of other plays. This movement underscores the significance of the waki's role in the play. The celebratory atmosphere calls for a faster tempo, and this shōdan is performed briskly, maintaining a forward energy that complements the theme of travel.
