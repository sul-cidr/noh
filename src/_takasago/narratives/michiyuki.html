Form
First Act - Waki Enters:
Netori Okitsuzumi → Kaikō → Nanori → Shidai → Michiyuki → Tsukizerifu
Synopsis
The priest and his two attendants are on the way to the capital.
Text
Poetry in regular rhythm.
Music
The chant is sung in tsuyogin style set in hiranori. It is accompanied by the percussion in strict rhythmic setting and a non-congruent nohkan part
Visuals
The waki and the two wakizure face each other are still standing at Square 5 and Square 2, respectively. The waki turns and faces front to announce that they have now arrived at Takasago Bay in Banshū.

Hashitomi’s shite is a young and elegant lady, so his slow exit is well in character. He exits the stage in silence, according to the tradition in the Morita School of nohkan featured here. However, when the shite is a deity, as it is the case in Kokaji, his exit is accompanied by music.
