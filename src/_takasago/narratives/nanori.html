First Act - Waki Enters:
Netori Okitsuzumi → Kaikō → Nanori → Shidai → Michiyuki → Tsukizerifu
Synopsis
The monk introduces himself, saying he is a priest from the Aso Shrine in Higo Province of Kyushu. He explains that he and his two acolytes are on their way to the capital, intending to stop at Takasago Bay in Banshū along the way.
Text
Prose.
Music
Unaccompanied vocal part of the waki delivered in kotoba.
Visuals
The waki stands at Square 9.

The monk introduces himself and explains his intentions in the simplest way, by speaking unaccompanied. The speech is tranquil, steady, and performed almost without movement while the actor remains on Square 1. Although the overall texture remains simple, the inclusion of the narrative that answers the 'who,' 'why,' 'what' and 'where' about the monk, gives this shōdan a higher Intermedia Index than the preceding Nanoribue.

The monk concludes the Nanori by bringing his hands together, holding a Buddhist rosary in the right hand. While the origin of this gesture is believed to be related to prayer, it has become a standard closing gesture also used by secular characters such as the Emperor’s envoy in Kokaji.
