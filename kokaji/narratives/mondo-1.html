First Act - Waki Enters:
Nanori → Mondō-1 → Ageuta-1 → Dialogue
Synopsis
The imperial envoy arrives at swordsmith Munechika’s house, where he delivers the sovereign's order to forge him a blade. Munechika replies that he cannot do this without a skilled assistant, but the envoy tells him he must.
Text
Prose.
Music
Unaccompanied exchange between the waki and wakizure delivered in kotoba.
Visuals
The action takes place on the stage's bridge, which represents the entrance to Munechika’s house. The wakizure (imperial envoy), standing at the First Pine, calls for Munechika. The curtain rises, and the waki (Munechika), dressed in "street clothes" of a samurai, walks in. He stops at the Third Pine, kneels, and bows to the wakizure. They remain in their positions throughout the ensuing dialogue.

With the introduction of a second actor, the overall dramatic intensity increases slightly as the action takes place on the bridge rather than on the main stage. The swordsmith Munechika greets the imperial envoy with a customary bow. What follows is an unmetered, unaccompanied dialogue in prose that keeps the Intermedia Index low.

After learning that he must forge a sword for the Emperor, Munechika, who is doubting his abilities, tries to find a way to decline the request. The envoy, getting impatient, delivers his last line: “Let me repeat the imperial command ”, in suteru, a style of forceful singing, stressing his order with a commanding step towards the swordsmith.
